Gym Sales Playbook

How to get gyms on board — a simple guide for the sales team

Internal Use Only

The Offer: Pilot Partner Program

We don't just "give free months." We invite gyms to become Pilot Partners. This makes them feel invested, filters out time-wasters, and gives us real feedback.

The deal in one sentence:
"We give you 3 months free. In return, you onboard your coaches, jump on a short monthly call with us, and let us use your story if you love it."

What the gym gets

  • 3 months completely free — no credit card, no commitment
  • Personal setup help — we configure their first coaches and clients
  • Direct line to our team — priority support during the pilot

What we ask in return

  • Onboard at least 3 coaches and 10 clients in the first month
  • A 30-minute feedback call once a month (that's it)
  • If they like the product, we can use their name & logo as a case study

Why This Works

1
Zero risk for the gym They don't pay anything and don't sign a contract. The only thing they "spend" is time setting up — which is exactly what gets them hooked.
2
Once the data is in, they stay After 3 months of workouts, client history, and progress photos, switching away feels like starting from scratch. The product retains itself.
3
The feedback obligation filters out freebie-hunters Someone who won't commit to a 30-min call was never going to pay. This keeps our pipeline clean.
4
Gyms talk to each other One happy gym owner in a neighborhood tells three others. Early pilots become our best marketing channel.

How to Talk to a Gym Owner

Keep it casual and confident. Here's a simple script you can adapt:

"Hey [Name], we're building a coaching platform that makes it super easy for your trainers to program workouts and track clients from their phone.

We're picking a handful of gyms in [city] to try it free for 3 months — no strings. All we ask is honest feedback so we can make it great.

The setup takes about 15 minutes — I can do it for you. Interested?"

After They Say Yes

Speed matters. The faster they see value, the more likely they convert.

1
Same day: Set up their account Create the gym, add their head coach, and load 2–3 sample workouts so it doesn't look empty.
2
Day 1–3: Help coaches add their first real clients Offer a quick 15-min video call or in-person walkthrough. Get at least one coach actively assigning workouts.
3
Week 2: Check in Quick text: "How's it going? Anything confusing?" Fix issues fast — this is when most drop-offs happen.
4
Month 1: First feedback call Ask what they love, what's missing, and whether their clients are engaging. Take notes — this is gold for the product team.
5
Month 3: Conversion conversation By now the data lock-in has done its job. Present pricing and ask which plan fits. Don't discount — the value should speak for itself.

Do's & Don'ts

DO

  • Offer to set up everything yourself
  • Follow up within 24 hours of a "yes"
  • Frame it as a partnership, not charity
  • Keep the pilot to 3 months max
  • Ask for a testimonial at the end
  • Target gyms with 3+ coaches (bigger potential)

DON'T

  • Give more than 3 months free
  • Offer big discounts after the pilot (anchors low value)
  • Skip the feedback calls (that's our intel)
  • Onboard a gym and then disappear
  • Waste time on gyms that won't commit to onboarding
  • Bad-mouth competitors — just be better
